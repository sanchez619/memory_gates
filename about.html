Types of Memory Gates


The Story Gate

Various stories and myths have stood the test of time. King Arthur, Heracles, Prince Siddharta... their tales were told in a way which made it possible to remember information even without written media. This phenomenon can be used to quickly remember many random things you require on a list. If you can let your mind create a story containing everything you need to know, you can skip writing a shopping list for good.

The Number Gate

Named after its creator, the Major Method is the most popular way to memorise numbers. You exchange number foe consonants and create small words or phrases. This way, even number with larger digits can stay in your mind. Do you see the number 4,617,521? What I see is a gate to the R(u)sh D(u)ck L(a)nd, a theme park made for duck races. Seems silly? But does it still seem silly when you remember important statistics with this method?

The Palace Gate

Around 500 years BC, the Greek philosopher Euripides achieved an astounding feat. In his mind, he was able to tell the whereabouts of all people inside a collapsed building. This way, he was able to find the remains of every person for the families to properly mourn and lay to rest. It is the first mention of a mind palace. Spatial awareness is the most basic of human skills and this technique uses this skill to link information with familiar locations. The mind palace can be used to store massive amounts of information for almost indefinitely, like one of the greatest literary characters, Sherlock Holmes, has proven time and time again.
